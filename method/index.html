Method

How eCPM Bazaar diagnoses mobile ad revenue drops.

Revenue is the final symptom. eCPM Bazaar first separates the movement into traffic, pricing, fill, country, placement, ad-source, and audience-timing signals so a small team knows what to check before changing floors or mediation settings.

Method

Revenue change is decomposed by

Impressions

Did ad exposure fall because DAU, sessions, placement visibility, or impressions per DAU changed?

Weighted eCPM

Did pricing change after accounting for country, format, placement, and ad-source mix?

Fill / match rate

Did requests stay stable while fills dropped, pointing to demand, mediation, floor, or availability issues?

Country mix

Did traffic shift toward lower-value GEOs, lowering blended eCPM even if each country stayed normal?

Placement exposure

Did one rewarded, interstitial, or banner placement stop showing enough impressions?

Ad source / mediation

Did one source or mediation setup contribute most of the change?

Audience timing / live events

Did sports matches, holidays, seasonality, or time-of-day behavior reduce impressions and distort daily averages?

Diagnosis order

Check upstream signals before trusting blended eCPM.

  1. 1Check impressions first
  2. 2Compare match rate / fill rate
  3. 3Split by country
  4. 4Split by placement or ad unit
  5. 5Check ad source / mediation source
  6. 6Check time-of-day and external events
  7. 7Only then look at blended eCPM

Time-of-day and external events

Audience behavior can look like an ad stack problem.

Revenue changes can be caused by audience behavior, not only ad demand. If the app normally monetizes most strongly during a specific time window, live sports, holidays, exams, work schedules, or local events can reduce impressions during the hours that matter most. This is why daily averages can be misleading. eCPM Bazaar encourages checking hourly or period-level patterns before assuming eCPM, floors, or mediation failed.

What the method does not claim

It is a directional CSV diagnosis, not final attribution. It does not replace AdMob, AppLovin MAX, Unity LevelPlay, TopOn, or your internal analytics. It does not guarantee higher revenue. It helps decide the first likely area to inspect.
